# Lab 2 — Data Exploration and Inferences

## Overview

This lab focuses on exploring relationships, trends, and patterns within air quality and agricultural datasets. The objective is to investigate long-term AQI trends, seasonal pollution behaviour, relationships between pollution and crop production, and communicate findings clearly to policymakers.

The tasks emphasize analytical reasoning, interpretation of data, and drawing evidence-based conclusions using visualisations and statistical relationships.

---

# Task 6 — AQI Trend Analysis Over Time

## Objective

A journalist wants to know whether air quality in India has improved, worsened, or remained unchanged since pollution control measures introduced after 2018.

## Requirements

* Extract and analyze the time dimension from the dataset
* Investigate AQI trends across the past eight years
* Identify the most polluted and least polluted years
* Select a visualization that clearly communicates long-term trends to a non-technical audience
* Interpret what the overall trend suggests about air quality changes over time

## Deliverables

* A visualization showing AQI trends across years
* Key years clearly highlighted
* A markdown response written in plain language summarizing the findings for the journalist

---

# Task 7 — Seasonal AQI Pattern Investigation

## Objective

An agricultural NGO claims that air pollution becomes significantly worse during the October–December harvest season due to crop residue burning.

## Requirements

* Investigate whether AQI follows a seasonal pattern during the year
* Determine the most suitable level of time aggregation for analysis
* Analyze AQI variation across months or seasons
* Evaluate whether the NGO’s claim is supported by the data

## Deliverables

* A visualization showing AQI variation across the year
* A markdown explanation responding directly to the NGO’s claim using evidence from the analysis

---

# Task 8 — Dataset Integration and Relationship Analysis

## Objective

The main research question is whether states with worse air quality also tend to produce lower crop output. Since both datasets were collected at different levels, they cannot be directly merged without transformation.

## Requirements

* Identify the transformations required to make both datasets compatible
* Prepare both datasets for merging
* Perform the merge successfully
* Explore relationships between all numerical variables in the combined dataset
* Identify and explain the two most significant or interesting relationships discovered

## Deliverables

* A markdown explanation describing the transformations required before merging
* A visualization showing relationships among numerical features
* Two relationship analyses with proposed real-world explanations

# Task 9 — Policy Briefing for the Environment Minister

## Objective

The State Environment Minister requires a short, non-technical summary of the findings before a cabinet meeting.

## Requirements

Prepare a clear and honest briefing that:

* Summarizes the three strongest findings from the analysis
* Explains what those findings mean in practical terms
* Recommends one action the government could take
* Clearly states at least one limitation of the analysis
* Avoids technical jargon and uses plain language throughout

## Deliverables

* A 150–200 word markdown briefing addressed directly to the minister
* Three findings
* One recommendation
* One honest limitation of the analysis

# Task B — Measuring the AQI–Crop Relationship

## Objective

## Requirements
